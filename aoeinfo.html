What is AoE?

Age of Empires is a series of historical real-time strategy video games, originally developed by Ensemble Studios and published by Xbox Game Studios.

Overview

The Age of Empires series immerses players in historical settings, allowing them to command civilizations across various time periods.
Players gather resources (such as wood, food, gold, and stone), build armies, create thriving economies, and wage wars against rivals to achieve domination.
The gameplay focuses on balancing economic growth, technological research, and military strength.

Gameplay Elements

Civilizations: Each game features a variety of unique civilizations, each with specific bonuses, units, and technologies, reflecting their historical traits.
Campaigns: The series includes detailed campaigns based on real historical figures and events, such as Joan of Arc, Genghis Khan, and the rise of the Roman Empire.
Multiplayer: Competitive and cooperative multiplayer modes have always been a cornerstone, with ranked matches and casual modes.
Resource Management: Players must manage resources wisely to ensure their economy supports both technological advancement and military efforts.
Technologies: Advancing through "Ages" (e.g., Feudal, Castle, Imperial) unlocks new units, buildings, and strategies.

Legacy and Impact

The Age of Empires series is praised for blending history and gaming. It's inspired countless players to learn about world history while mastering strategic gameplay.
The community is vibrant, with active modding, tournaments, and ongoing updates from the developers.
It has set a gold standard for RTS games and remains a cornerstone of the genre.

Key Titles in the Series

Age of Empires (1997)

An Epic Game of Empire-Building and Conquest. How will you rule the world? Conquer enemy civilizations? Discover the world's secrets? Accumulate wealth? As the guiding sprit of a Stone Age tribe, follow one of these paths to build the greatest civilization on earth.
Age of Empires™, choose your favorite form of world domination. -Microsoft
The first game in the series set the foundation for RTS gaming, focusing on ancient civilizations like Egypt, Greece, and Mesopotamia. It covered the Stone Age to the Iron Age, emphasizing technological progression and historical events.

Age of Empires: The Rise of Rome (Expansion, October 29, 1998)

Experience all the conflict, intrigue, greed, and power as you create the most feared and celebrated civilization in history, The Roman Empire.
Created by award-winning real time strategy game, Age of Empires©,The Rise of Rome lets you play an active role in directing the ascension of the infamous Roman Empire. -Microsoft
This expansion introduced Roman civilization and new campaigns, units, and technologies.

Age of Empires II: The Age of Kings (1999)

One of the most beloved entries, it focuses on the medieval era, featuring civilizations like the Franks, Britons, and Mongols. Its strategic depth, historical campaigns, and balanced gameplay remain iconic.
Expansion: The Conquerors (2000) added new civilizations (e.g., Aztecs, Mayans), campaigns, and units.

Age of Mythology (2002)

A spin-off of the series, focusing on mythological rather than historical themes. Players command civilizations like the Greeks, Egyptians, and Norse while using gods and mythical creatures in battle.

Age of Empires III (2005)

Set in the colonial era, it introduced new gameplay mechanics, such as "Home Cities," which provided strategic shipments to aid players. It focused on European powers like Spain, Britain, and France as they expanded into the New World.
Expansion packs: The WarChiefs (2006) and The Asian Dynasties (2007) introduced Native American and Asian civilizations.

Age of Empires Online (2011)

An online multiplayer-focused game that brought a stylized art style and featured cooperative missions. While innovative, it was short-lived due to its shift in gameplay direction.

Age of Empires II: Definitive Edition (2019)

A remastered version with improved graphics, new content, and modernized mechanics while retaining the spirit of the original. It includes several expansions, adding new civilizations like the Burgundians and Sicilians.

Age of Empires IV (2021)

The latest mainline game, revisiting the medieval period with enhanced graphics and refined gameplay. It introduced asymmetric civilizations, such as the Mongols and the Delhi Sultanate, which play uniquely.
It also offers rich historical storytelling through campaigns.
